Welcome Dome

Community Transition Centers provide temporary shelter, work training, and counseling services for people transitioning through major life changes. They are the perfect solution for helping the homeless or anyone else stuck in poverty, who feels the American dream has passed them by.

Community Transition Centers are split into two areas -- a public area, with lots of community events and a residents only area. The center includes a computer lab, coworking space, donation center, dining hall, community garden, showers, bathrooms, laundry, lounges, WiFi, carshare, bikeshare, and 24/7 on-site security.

Transition Centers are just a concept, but we believe they will be a critical innovation in solving homelessness across America. We're currently looking for land in California to launch a temporary pilot program, somewhere inside a major metropolitan area. Contact us if you can help.

    FAQs

    Why do cities need Transition Centers?

    Currently, there is a massive lack of supportive options for people who become homeless. Homeless individuals have two main options: shelters or the street --both are terrible, unsupportive environments for anyone who wants to regain a stable job and housing. On top of that, it takes many cities 4 weeks before shelter beds open up. When you make it in the shelter, everyone is thrown together -- the mentally ill, the addicted, sex offenders, you name it. This makes it very hard for people to stay motivated around others who may have very different goals, challenges, and focus. It also makes it incredibly challenging for shelters to properly accomodate these different groups. And because of this, neighborhoods fight tooth and nail to keep shelters away from their area.

    Bring this all together and you see how a new solution is needed. One that helps people get thier lives back on track, provides a positive impact on the neighborhood around it, and can be built at a low cost.

    How many people can stay in a Transtion Center?

    Transition Centers can shelter up to 125 people. After that point it gets hard to foster the supportive relationships and culture that we'd like this solution to foster.For the pilot program we want to run, we're planning on having 60 people at first, getting everything in order, and scaling up eventually when things feel right.

    How fast can a Transition Center be built?

    A center can be built in under 30 days. Each structure we use can be assembled or disassembled in a few hours. The biggest time-suck is waiting for the supplies after you order them.

    What will it take to open a Transition Center?

    To open a center, your community will need three things -- Community Support, Funding ($200k), and land.

    Where can Transition Centers be placed?

    Transition Centers can be placed on any large plot of land that isn't currently being used. Because all structures are temporary, everything in the camp can be taken down and moved to a new location in couple days if there is ever another need for the land they are on. Ideal locations include unused plots of land, rarely used parking lots, land awaiting development, unused city land, and church land.

    Has anything like a Transition Center ever been done before?

    There are over a dozen similar homeless camps built around the US, but nothing exactly like Transition Centers. This will be the first facility built for the purpose of helping people heal, learn new skills, and transition back out into the world. With this center, we are bringing together the best practices from multiple different organizations and decades of expereince in similar concepts.

    How much will it cost?

    Community Transition Centers are extreameley affordable, costing under $200,000 for a 100 small domes, 8 community domes, a computer lab, and all other supplies. Because all the structures we use are temporary and movable, we are able to avoid a lot of major shelter costs, which typically make caring for the homeless unaffordable.

    For on-going cost, our goal is to make the Transition Centers fully sustainable. Residents will also pay rent to stay in a Transition Center on a sliding scale. Those who can not afford it ($200/month), will be asked to contribute to community service projects, or work in one of the mulitple programs we plan to have on site.

    Do Transition Centers have any rules?

    Transition centers want to be a healthy and happy learning environment for everyone - for its residents and its neighbors. Therefore transition centers are drug and alcohol free zones and residents are required to follow a set of mutually agreed upon standards of behaviors that does not tolerate disruptive behavior, violence or discrimination of any type.

    For our pilot program, we will not be taking in homeless individuals suffering from substance abuse or severe mental illness (roughly 50% of the homeless population). We beleive traditional shelters and respit centers are better preparred for this group.
